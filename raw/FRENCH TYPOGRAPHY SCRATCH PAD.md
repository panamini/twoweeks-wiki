

Goal:
Write clean, correct, consistent French typography.

Core rules:
1. Do not put a space before commas or periods.
   Correct: Bonjour, merci.
   Correct: Fin de phrase.

2. Put a non-breaking space before:
   - colon :
   - semicolon ;
   - question mark ?
   - exclamation mark !
   Ideally use a thin non-breaking space before ; ? !

3. Use French guillemets:
   « text »
   Put a non-breaking space:
   - after the opening guillemet
   - before the closing guillemet
   Correct: Il a dit : « bonjour ! »

4. Apostrophes take no spaces.
   Correct: l’homme, j’aime, aujourd’hui

5. Hyphens take no spaces.
   Correct: peut-être, arc-en-ciel

6. Distinguish hyphen from dash.
   - hyphen: joins words
   - dash: marks interruption, parenthetical insertion, dialogue, or emphasis
   In running text, a dash is generally spaced on both sides according to French typographic convention.

7. Ellipsis is exactly three dots...
   - no space before
   - normally one space after
   Do not use two dots or four dots unless required by punctuation logic.

8. Keep accents and cedilla on capitals.
   Correct: É, À, È, Ù, Ç
   Never drop them in polished French.

9. Use italics for titles of works and publications.
   Do not overuse italics for simple emphasis.

10. In titles of works, capitalize only the first word by default.
   Then set the full title in italics.
   Example: Le rouge et le noir

11. Days and months take lowercase.
   Correct: lundi, avril
   Correct date style: le 15 avril 2026

12. Write time with spaces around h in careful typography.
   Correct: 14 h 30
   Avoid: 14h30 in polished typographic output

13. Use spaces to group large numbers by thousands.
   Correct: 50 000
   For four-digit numbers, spacing may depend on context.

14. Use a comma as the decimal separator.
   Correct: 3,14

15. Put a non-breaking space between a number and a unit symbol.
   Correct: 8 kg, 20 °C, 14 h
   Incorrect: 8kg, 20°C, 14h

16. Courtesy abbreviations:
   - M. for Monsieur
   - Mme without period
   - Mlle without period
   In direct address, prefer full forms:
   Monsieur,
   Madame,

17. Preserve ligatures when they belong to standard spelling.
   Correct: œuvre, cœur, Œdipe, curriculum vitæ

18. Keep punctuation style consistent across the whole document.
   Do not mix French and English spacing conventions.

Priority rules:
- no space before comma or period
- non-breaking space before : ; ? !
- French guillemets: « »
- accents on capitals
- comma for decimals
- non-breaking space between number and unit

Consistency rule:
Once French typography is chosen, keep all punctuation, spacing, quotation, capitalization, and number formatting fully French throughout the output.

Preferred default:
Use formal modern French typography with:
- French guillemets « »
- non-breaking spaces before : ; ? !
- accents on capitals
- italics for titles
- comma decimals
- spaced units
