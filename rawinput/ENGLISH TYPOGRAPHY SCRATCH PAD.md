Goal:
Write clean, consistent English typography.

First choose one variant and stay consistent:
- US English
- UK English

Core rules:
1. Do not use French spacing before punctuation.
   Write punctuation tight to the preceding word.

2. Use curly quotes, not straight quotes, in polished output.
   - US default: outer double quotes “ ”, inner single quotes ‘ ’
   - UK default: outer single quotes ‘ ’ or house-style double quotes “ ”
   - Keep one system throughout the document.

3. Quote punctuation:
   - US: commas and periods usually go inside closing quotation marks.
   - US: colons and semicolons stay outside closing quotation marks.
   - UK: use house style; punctuation is often logical and depends on whether it belongs to the quoted material.
   - Question marks and exclamation marks go inside only if they belong to the quote.

4. Apostrophes take no spaces.
   Use them for contractions and possession.

5. Use hyphens for compound modifiers before a noun.
   Example: a well-known author
   Do not usually hyphenate after -ly adverbs.
   Example: a highly respected author

6. Use dashes deliberately:
   - en dash (–) for ranges when the style allows it: 2019–2024
   - em dash (—) for interruption or emphasis
   - spacing around dashes depends on house style; do not mix styles

7. Titles and emphasis:
   - italicize long works: books, journals, films, newspapers
   - use quotation marks for short works: articles, chapters, poems, episodes
   - avoid overusing italics for emphasis

8. Capitalization:
   - use sentence case for UI, web, and plain-language content unless a style guide requires otherwise
   - use title case only when the chosen style guide calls for it
   - do not randomly capitalize common nouns

9. Dates:
   - spell out the month name to avoid ambiguity
   - US format: January 5, 2026
   - UK format: 5 January 2026
   - avoid ordinal endings in technical/plain-language prose: January 5, not January 5th

10. Time:
   - use numerals
   - US default: 2:00 PM
   - international/technical contexts may use 14:00
   - include time zone when relevant

11. Numbers:
   - default rule: write zero to nine in words, 10 and above in numerals
   - if the domain is technical, tabular, UI, or data-heavy, prefer numerals more often
   - never mix number styles arbitrarily in the same context

12. Units:
   - leave a space between the number and the SI unit symbol
   - examples: 7 m, 31.4 kg, 37 °C

13. Consistency rule:
   Once a style choice is made (US/UK, quote style, title case/sentence case, dash spacing, date format),
   keep it consistent across the entire output.

Preferred defaults when no style is specified:
- Variant: US English for general international professional prose
- Quotes: curly double outer quotes
- Capitalization: sentence case for web/UI, title case only for formal titles
- Dates: spell out month names
- Dashes: em dash for interruption, en dash for numeric ranges
